Span & Span Shift

Span shift compensation can be accomplished by three methods. The methods differ in the accuracy of the final result and in the complexity of data entry requirements. The simplest first-order correction is Span Shift with Temperature Change, Two Points. It requires entry of transducer span at two different temperatures and uses average TCR values for the Rmod resistor material. It assumes linear span shift with temperature change for the transducer, as well as linear TCR of the resistor material. This is rarely true, however, so it is common to have a slight nonlinear compensation with this method. When these nonlinearities prove to be excessive, we suggest trying the other two compensations: Span Shift with Temperature Change, Three Points and Span Shift with Temperature Change, Optimum Shunt.
