Inputs (Allowed Range)

Applied Force, F (0.000 to 20,000 lbf [0 to 88,960 N])

Force applied as a point load normal to the beam on its centerline, or as evenly distributed load along a line transverse to the centerline.

Width, w (0.020 to 10 in [0.51 to 254 mm])

Prismatic beam of uniform width in the gaged area.

Thickness, t (0.003 to 5 in [0.076 to 127 mm])

Prismatic beam of uniform thickness in the gaged area. Cannot exceed the beam width.

Load-Point-to-Grid-CL Length, L (0.100 to 100 in [2.54 to 2540 mm])

Measured. Should exceed twice the beam width.

Modulus of Elasticity (5E+6 to +50E+6 psi [34.5 to 345 GPa])

For the material from which the beam is constructed.

Gage Length (0.008 to 1.0 in [0.203 to 25.4 mm])

Obtained from strain gage catalog.

Gage Factor (1.0 to 5.0) The default value is 2.00.

From Engineering Data Form supplied with gages.

Calculated Values (Units)

Pressing the COMPUTE BUTTON after entering the variables (within their acceptable ranges) calculates the following three output values:

Nominal Gage Strain (microstrain)

The average of the absolute value of the strains in the surface of the beam under the four gages, when installed as shown in the drawing above. Ideally in the strain range of 1000 to 1700 microstrain at rated load.

Strain Variation (%)

Calculation of how much the strain varies under the active gage length. It is computed as a percentage from the difference in the maximum and minimum strains, divided by the maximum strain and multiplied times 100. Ideally under 15% variation.

Span at Applied Force (mV/V)

Calculated output of a full-bridge circuit with gages installed as shown in the above drawing, when the force is equal to the value entered. The mV/V units are millivolts of Wheatstone bridge output per volt of bridge excitation.

Error Messages

ï¿½Beam Thickness exceeds Beam Widthï¿½

Prevents entering a thickness that is greater than the beam width. When beams are "oversquare" (thickness exceeds width), they are more sensitive to lateral forces. This can lead to bucking under load.

ï¿½The calculated nominal gage strain exceeds 1700 microstrain, which is considered to be the maximum strain level recommended for normal full scale transducer operation.ï¿½

A value of 1700 microstrain is considered to be the maximum strain level recommended for normal transducers. However, TransCalc will calculate the nominal gage strain until the value reaches 5000 microstrain.

ï¿½Design Impracticable. Gage strain exceeds 5000 microstrainï¿½

No common transducer material will survive 5000 microstrain without an unacceptable amount of permanent zero offset. Accordingly, no calculations are made for bridge output. Adjust inputs so that nominal gage strain is less than 5000 microstrain.

"The Gage Length divided by 2 is greater than the distance from Load to Gage CL."

Checks to ensure that the gage length does not extend beyond the load point.
