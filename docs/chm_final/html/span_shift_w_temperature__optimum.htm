General Considerations

Span Shift with Temperature Change

Span Shift Compensation techniques

In span shift with temperature change, optimum shunt, data is taken in the same manner as 3 points except the values for Rtl, Rta, Rth are now labeled Rmod because the Rmod must be the actual value to be used in the finished transducer. Choosing the ambient temperature Rmod value is sometimes by trial and error or a matter of convenience due to the availability of certain standard resistance values. Since the Rmod will be shunted, it will typically be 20-50% higher in ambient temperature value than the Rmod calculated in span shift with temperature change, 2 points.

Inputs

Span at High, Ambient & Low Temperature Three measured values from the transducer under test are entered at three test temperatures. The actual temperatures used are not critical providing that the temperatures are separated by a reasonable amount. For example, a transducer which is designed to operate over a 0ï¿½F to 120ï¿½F temperature range would typically be tested at 0ï¿½F, 75ï¿½F and 120ï¿½F to obtain the best accuracy of compensation.

The bridge output entered is the output at test load minus the bridge zero output at the same temperature. Span measurement should not be done with Rmod and Rspan resistors in the circuit. If these resistors are already wired into the bridge circuit, they can be temporarily disabled by shorting across their terminals.

Rbridge The bridge resistance value measured at P+ and P- for each of the three temperatures. The measurement should not include span set or span shift with temperature change resistors.

Rmod The resistance value of Rmod measured at each of the three temperatures. Rmod must be installed on the spring material for these measurements.

Solutions

Rshunt

The value of shunt resistor to be placed in parallel with Rmod.

Span

The calculated span at the same three temperatures as used for data entry. Any nonlinearities remaining are clearly evident.

Select

Select Rmod resistor type from the available choices.
