B-Pattern Resistors

Circuits Refinements command

B-Pattern resistors have two primary uses. The most common is to provide an adjustable nickel resistor for experimentally proving the optimum resistance value for span shift with temperature compensation before ordering a custom A-Pattern resistor. B-Pattern resistors are rarely used for production because of the time required to adjust them to a specific value.

The second use of B-Pattern resistors is for setting the span of a transducer. Depending upon the pattern selected, a fixed, stable value of resistance anywhere between 200 and nearly zero ohms can be obtained through careful shorting of the parallel lines. To minimize the temperature coefficient of resistance when installed on the transducer surface, these resistors have grids of constantan alloy. They are available in 06 and 13 STC for elements of aluminum alloys and steel, respectively.

Adjustment Techniques

The B Pattern bondable resistor is adjusted (decrease in resistance) by shorting out the parallel conducting lines at a desired point along their length. Depending on required stability, shorting can be accomplished through use of a suitable solder or a conductive silver compound.
